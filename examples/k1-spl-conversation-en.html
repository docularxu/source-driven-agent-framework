Retrospective Summary
📋 Retrospective: 5 Key Lessons Learned
1. Researcher fabricated content by going out of scope (most severe)
Agent examined HEAD instead of patch diff, writing content from other patches into the current analysis. Mitigation: explicitly require "git show" for diffs in task descriptions.
2. Lost notifications caused workflow stalls (×2)
Subagent completion notifications were not delivered, leaving the Architect unaware that tasks had finished. An inherent flaw of the passive session model.
3. Lack of observability
Initially no progress.md or comms.md existed - the human had no way to track inter-Agent interactions. Added after human pointed out the gap.
4. Cascade risk from parallel scheduling
When review finds issues, downstream modules may have already been analyzed based on incorrect conclusions. Strategy: parallel for weak dependencies, serial for strong ones.
5. "File exists ≠ task complete"
Agent incorrectly assumed file existence equals task completion. Human corrected: must check subagents list for authoritative status.
